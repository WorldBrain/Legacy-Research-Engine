Data & Privacy

  • Who has access to my data?

    NOBODY. Everything is stored and processed locally and nothing ever leaves your computer without your prior approval.
    In later stages you can voluntarily and modularly share metadata with anybody you like. (friends, organisations, researchers, data scientists...or WorldBrain)
    This will be a completely open ecosystem in which you as a user are fully in control over how your data is used and by whom.

    Don't believe us? That's the spirit...always stay critical.
    Our code is open source and you can see what is running on your computer by checking our GithHub repository or the data on your local filesystem.

  • What kind of data is currently stored and processed?

    User-unrelated data
    • - url
    • - title of content
    • - content body text

    User-related data
    • - LastVisitTime of url

    How is the data analysed?
    • - building the search index

  • How much data is used on my system?

    The plugin needs about 10-15kb of storage for each url you visit.

    If you are a heavy web user and visit 5000 pages per month, it needs about 75Mb of additional storage each month.

  • Does the extension slow down my browsing?

    The plugin constantly blocks between 30-40MB of RAM, even in stress situations.
    This should not influence your performance.
    As a comparison, each tab running Facebook tab needs around 300MB.

    If you run into any problem, don't hesitate to leave a quick message at info@worldbrain.io and we get back to you as soon as possible.

About the WorldBrain project

  • Who is behind all this?

    We are a team 10 people consisting of Oliver Sauter, the founder of the project, and 9 contributors that help the project out in their free time.
    In our contributor team we cover a wide range of expertise, from Full-Stack-Development, Dev-Ops, Machine Learning and Security.

    As a team we value critical thinking and deeply care about improving our greatest invention, the internet, by making it easier to sift through this vast amount of information we encounter on a daily basis.

  • Who is backing this financially?

    Currently, the project is privately funded by Oliver Sauter, but now that we have our extension out, we are looking for grant and crowd-funding to cover our expenses. You can support us on Patreon with a monthly donation of your choice.
